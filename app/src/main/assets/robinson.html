Robinson Road to Birds Landing

This area is southwest of Sacramento in Solano County.  It is especially good birding in the winter, when raptors can be found among the open grasslands, and clusters of eucalyptus and Osage orange trees. This area is excellent for wintering Mountain Plovers (check Central Valley Bird Club listserv for latest reports:  groups.yahoo.com/group/

central_valley_birds), and for Loggerhead Shrikes year-round.  Traffic is sparse along these quiet dirt roads, which helps make birding pleasant and rewarding.  The best times to bird are from October to February, although at times it can be foggy.    

Birding the site

Ferruginous Hawks, Rough-legged Hawks (numbers vary year to year) and Red-tailed Hawks are quite reliable during the winter.  Bald and Golden Eagles are occasionally found, especially around the large power lines also frequented by Prairie Falcons.  Watch the fields for large flocks of Mountain Plovers and Horned Larks.  Other species found in this area are Long-billed Curlew, Loggerhead Shrikes, American Pipits, Burrowing Owls, Great Horned Owls, Merlins, Say’s Phoebes, Western Bluebirds and Savannah Sparrows.  Watch the ponds for wintering waterfowl and geese.  Highlights in this area have included Gyrfalcon and Northern Shrike.
