William Land Park

“Although most of the area south of William Land Park has been taken over as a residential area, the birding is surprisingly good in the few remaining natural spots. Probably the best is the “panhandle” along Riverside Boulevard between 11th and 13th Avenues.

Birding the site

Large conifers, berry-laden shrubs and trees attract winter flocks of American Robin, Cedar Waxwing, Yellow-rumped Warbler, Dark-eyed Junco, and American Goldfinch. Red-breasted Sapsucker is usually present and Red-naped and Yellow-bellied Sapsuckers have been seen in the area. Hutton’s Vireo can usually be found in the evergreen oaks. This is as good a place as any in Sacramento to look for wintering Varied Thrush (under the redwoods), Red-breasted Nuthatch, Golden-crowned Kinglet, Townsend’s Warbler, Purple Finch and Pine Siskin.

The riverside trails in winter may reveal a Mew Gull flying over the river or a Winter Wren calling from a blackberry thicket. A spring hike will produce migrating warblers, grosbeaks and tanagers. Breeding birds in this area include Wood Duck, White-tailed Kite, Red-shouldered and Swainson’s Hawks, American Kestrel, Great Horned Owl and Western Screech-Owl, Nuttall’s and Downy Woodpeckers, Belted Kingfisher and Tree Swallow.”
