About BirdWalk

BirdWalk is designed as a companion app to the Sacramento Audubon Society’s (SAS) “Birding in the Sacramento Region”. Content about each trail is excerpted directly from this book. BirdWalk supplements the SAS’s teaching and community outreach about birds and where to see them.

Identifying Birds

Each site lists several birds that are likely to be seen at that location. For help identifying a bird you have spotted, you can check out the SAS's web site or consult “Birding In the Sacramento Region”. Other helpful apps for bird identification include Audubon's Birds of North America, Sibley Birds of North America, BirdsEye Bird Finding Guide, or Merlin Bird ID by Cornell Lab America.

Operational Issues, Safety and Liability

Operational issues such as park fees and hours and general safety precautions are mentioned in the “Read More About…” page. Sacramento Audubon Society's mission:

a) promote the protection and scientific study of wild birds

b) promote the enjoyment and appreciation of wild birds through community outreach

c) provide, encourage and support environmental educational opportunities

d) provide proactive leadership in the conservation of open space in the Sacramento region

The Sacramento Audubon Society is an incorporated 501c3 non profit organization located in the Central Valley of Northern California. BirdWalk’s content is based on the Sacramento Audubon Society’s “Birding in the Sacramento Region”, Fourth Edition, Sacramento Audubon Society © 2008.
