Sherman Island, Brannan Island, Korth’s Marina, Eddo’s RV Park                        

This seldom visited portion of Sacramento County, including Sherman Island and Brannan Island, has a lot of potential. There can be thousands of waterfowl, and many raptors, shorebirds, sparrows, and blackbirds. Recent surprise finds have included Brant, Ruff, Cassin's Kingbird, and Common Grackle. It is typically most productive (and visited) in winter, but has potential year-round. As one would expect from an area popular with wind surfers, it is often very windy, which can impede birding. Other delta “islands” are worth exploring.

                                                                        

Birding the site

A good place to check on the way to Sherman Island is Korth’s Marina on Brannan Island Road. After crossing the Mokelumne River, 10 miles from Interstate 5, turn right on Brannan Island Road (watch for “Delta Loop” signs). Follow the road south under the highway for 3.0 miles. Park and check the trees. This place has all the makings of a migrant trap, and is worth checking especially in migration and winter. Continue on the Delta Loop to reach Highway 160. Parking is limited, but check the San Joaquin River along the way.

                                                

If you skip the Delta Loop, Highway 160 is 15 miles west of Interstate 5 via Highway 12. Turn left. Brannan Island State Recreation Area ($6) is 3.4 miles south on Highway 160. There is a good mix of trees, water, and open areas. Continuing south on 160, it is 6.5 miles south of Highway 12 to West Sherman Island Road. Turn right, and in 3.5 miles you will reach the county park ($5). Along the way and at the park, there are good views of the Sacramento River. At the park, there have been breeding Yellow-breasted Chats. Backtracking to Highway 160, it is 1.5 miles south to Sherman Island Rd. Don’t miss this road; next stop is the Antioch toll bridge. Turn left and follow this up onto the levee, scanning fields, fence lines, and trees along the way. The levee road leads to Eddo’s RV Park—another spot worth checking.
